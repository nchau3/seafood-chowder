FAMOUS CHOWDER

INGREDIENTS

  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 2-3 medium onions, diced
  • 1/2 cup dulse, chopped
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 1 tetrapack chicken broth (946ml)
  • 3 bottles clam juice
  • 2-3 medium potatoes, cubed
  • 1-2 carrots, diced
  • 2-3 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 cup 35% cream (approximate)

SPICES

  • salt
  • oregano
  • dill weed
  • bay leaves (5-6)
  • cajun seasoning (optional)

SEAFOOD

  • 5-7 frozen haddock fillets (whole)
  • 3-5 frozen salmon fillets (cubed)
  • 15-20 frozen shrimp, shelled/deveined (chopped or whole)
  • 1 can baby clams
  1. In a large saucepan, melt butter over medium heat. Add onions, salt and dulse.
  2. When onions are starting to brown and stick to the pan, deglaze with white wine. Allow most of the alcohol to burn off.
  3. Add chicken broth and clam juice. Refill tetrapack and bottles once each with water and add to pot.
  4. Add oregano, dillweed and bay leaves (in a filter basket or coffee filter/teabag), add potatoes, then simmer and reduce for 30-45 minutes.
  5. Add carrots and celery, then continue simmering vegetables have reached desired consistency.
  6. Add cream to pot, bring to boil. Remove from heat and add 1 or more seafood options + cajun seasoning if desired.
  7. Serve with a pinch of paprika and a side of garlic bread.

TIPS

  • seafood should be defrosted just enough to slice, it will be cooked with residual heat from the broth
  • timing for adding vegetables depends on what you like (add closer to the end for more crunch)
